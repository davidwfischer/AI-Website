Types

ELIZA was the first chatbot program, developed in 1966 by Joseph Weizenbaum. It was able to hold simple conversations and respond to certain keywords. ELIZA was a milestone in the development of chatbots and influenced many later models.

SHRDLU was an early example of a natural language processing system developed by Terry Winograd in 1970. It could understand natural language and respond to instructions to perform simple tasks in a virtual world.

BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) is a large language model developed by Google in 2018. Unlike previous language models that only consider the previous text, BERT can consider both the previous and subsequent text.

GPT-3 (Generative Pre-trained Transformer 3) is one of the largest language models developed by OpenAI. It has 175 billion parameters and has demonstrated impressive capabilities in natural language understanding and generation.

Google BARD(Bidirectional & Autoregressive Decoder) is a language model designed to have natural conversations with users. It is trained on a wide range of topics and can provide informative and engaging responses.
